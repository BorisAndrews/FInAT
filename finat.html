class finat.ast.LeviCivita(free, bound, body)[source]¶

Bases: pymbolic.primitives._MultiChildExpression

The Levi-Civita symbol expressed as an operator.

Parameters:
  • free – A tuple of free indices.
  • bound – A tuple of indices over which to sum.
  • body – The summand.

The length of free + bound must be exactly 3. The Levi-Civita operator then represents the summation over the bound indices of the Levi-Civita symbol times the body. For example in the case of two bound indices:

\[\mathrm{LeviCivita((\alpha,), (\beta, \gamma), body)} = \sum_{\beta,\gamma}\epsilon_{\alpha,\beta,\gamma} \mathrm{body}\]

finat.vectorfiniteelement module¶

class finat.vectorfiniteelement.VectorFiniteElement(element, dimension)[source]¶

Bases: finat.finiteelementbase.FiniteElementBase

A Finite element whose basis functions have the form:

\[\boldsymbol\phi_{\beta,i} = \mathbf{e}_{\beta}\phi_i\]

Where \(\{\mathbf{e}_\beta,\, \beta=0\ldots\mathrm{dim}\}\) is the basis for \(\mathbb{R}^{\mathrm{dim}}\) and \(\{\phi_i\}\) is the basis for the corresponding scalar finite element space.

Parameters:
  • element – The scalar finite element.
  • dimension – The geometric dimension of the vector element.

\(\boldsymbol\phi_{i,\beta}\) is, of course, vector-valued. If we subscript the vector-value with \(\alpha\) then we can write:

\[\boldsymbol\phi_{\alpha,(i,\beta)} = \delta_{\alpha,\beta}\phi_i\]

This form enables the simplification of the loop nests which will eventually be created, so it is the form we employ here.

basis_evaluation(points, kernel_data, derivative=None, pullback=True)[source]¶

Produce the recipe for basis function evaluation at a set of points \(q\):

\[\boldsymbol\phi_{\alpha (i \beta) q} = \delta_{\alpha \beta}\phi_{i q}\]\[\nabla\boldsymbol\phi_{(\alpha \gamma) (i \beta) q} = \delta_{\alpha \beta}\nabla\phi_{\gamma i q}\]\[\nabla\times\boldsymbol\phi_{(i \beta) q} = \epsilon_{2 \beta \gamma}\nabla\phi_{\gamma i q} \qquad\textrm{(2D)}\]\[\nabla\times\boldsymbol\phi_{\alpha (i \beta) q} = \epsilon_{\alpha \beta \gamma}\nabla\phi_{\gamma i q} \qquad\textrm{(3D)}\]\[\nabla\cdot\boldsymbol\phi_{(i \beta) q} = \nabla\phi_{\beta i q}\]

Produce the recipe for the evaluation of a field f at a set of points \(q\):

\[\boldsymbol{f}_{\alpha q} = \sum_i f_{i \alpha}\phi_{i q}\]\[\nabla\boldsymbol{f}_{\alpha \beta q} = \sum_i f_{i \alpha}\nabla\phi_{\beta i q}\]\[\nabla\times\boldsymbol{f}_{q} = \epsilon_{2 \beta \gamma}\sum_{i} f_{i \beta}\nabla\phi_{\gamma i q} \qquad\textrm{(2D)}\]\[\nabla\times\boldsymbol{f}_{\alpha q} = \epsilon_{\alpha \beta \gamma}\sum_{i} f_{i \beta}\nabla\phi_{\gamma i q} \qquad\textrm{(3D)}\]\[\nabla\cdot\boldsymbol{f}_{q} = \sum_{i \alpha} f_{i \alpha}\nabla\phi_{\alpha i q}\]
moment_evaluation(value, weights, points, kernel_data, derivative=None, pullback=True)[source]¶

Produce the recipe for the evaluation of the moment of \(u_{\alpha,q}\) against a test function \(v_{\beta,q}\).

\[\int \boldsymbol{u} \cdot \boldsymbol\phi_{(i \beta)}\ \mathrm{d}x = \sum_q \boldsymbol{u}_{\beta q}\phi_{i q}w_q\]\[\int \boldsymbol{u}_{(\alpha \gamma)} \nabla \boldsymbol\phi_{(\alpha \gamma) (i \beta)}\ \mathrm{d}x = \sum_{\gamma q} \boldsymbol{u}_{(\beta \gamma) q}\nabla\phi_{\gamma i q}w_q\]\[\int u \nabla \times \boldsymbol\phi_{(i \beta)}\ \mathrm{d}x = \sum_{q} u_{q}\epsilon_{2\beta\gamma}\nabla\phi_{\gamma i q}w_q \qquad\textrm{(2D)}\]\[\int u_{\alpha} \nabla \times \boldsymbol\phi_{\alpha (i \beta)}\ \mathrm{d}x = \sum_{\alpha q} u_{\alpha q}\epsilon_{\alpha\beta\gamma}\nabla\phi_{\gamma i q}w_q \qquad\textrm{(3D)}\]\[\int u \nabla \cdot \boldsymbol\phi_{(i \beta)}\ \mathrm{d}x = \sum_{q} u_q\nabla\phi_{\beta i q}w_q\]

Appropriate code is also generated where the value contains test functions.
